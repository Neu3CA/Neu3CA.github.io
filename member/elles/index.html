Biography

My degree is in Electrical Engineering with focal points on computational mathematics and biomedical engineering. My master thesis is about hyperthermia by means of electromagnetic waves, which is a field that is closely related to High Intensity Focused Ultrasound (HIFU). The project included modelling of tissues in the sense of wave propagation and temperature increase as well as designing the antennas of the hyperthermia device. Thus I have some experience in the design and measurement areas, as well as computational and more theoretical sides of the engineering field.

I am a fast learner, broadly oriented and interested in many topics, including science, philosphy, languages and arts. I own a mug with a picture of Gudetama on it. I consider problems a challenge and am willing to dig deeply into a subject to come up with a solution. Because of my many interests, I tend to find creative solutions and combine solutions from a variety of topics.

My PhD project is about the mechanism of neurostimulation in general and Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation in general. I aim to find out which electromagnetic waveforms are most suitable to modulate the activity of neuronal networks, and how varies sub-types of neurons may be selectively stimulated.
